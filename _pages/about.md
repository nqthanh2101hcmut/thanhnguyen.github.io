Welcome to my homepage! I am Thanh Nguyen, a graduate researcher in Applied Mathematics at Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (VNU-HCM).

My research lies broadly in the mathematical foundations of generative models. I am particularly interested in objective design, density estimation, score-based learning, and the theoretical principles behind modern generative methods such as diffusion models and normalizing flows.

Currently, I work under the supervision of Dr. Le Xuan Dai on improving training objectives for generative models. More broadly, I am interested in connections between generative modeling, optimal transport, and Schrödinger bridge methods, with the goal of developing learning algorithms that are both mathematically principled and practically useful.

Previously, I also worked on Bayesian optimization for machine learning. Alongside my graduate studies, I am pursuing a second degree in Mathematics at Dalat University.
