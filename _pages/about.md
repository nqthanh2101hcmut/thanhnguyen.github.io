Welcome to my homepage! I am **Thanh Nguyen**, a graduate researcher in **Applied Mathematics** at **Ho Chi Minh University of Technology - VNU HCMC**. My work is broadly interested in the **mathematical foundations and methodology of generative models**, especially how to build objectives and solvers that are not only effective in practice but also theoretically well-grounded.

Currently, I work as a **Graduate Research Assistant** under the supervision of **Dr. Le Xuan Dai**, where I study how **density estimation**, **score matching**, and related optimization principles can be used to improve the training objectives of generative models. More specifically, I am interested in understanding the trade-offs between stability, convergence, and sample quality across frameworks such as **diffusion models**, **normalizing flows**, and other flow/score-based approaches.

More broadly, my research explores the connection between **generative modeling** and mathematical tools such as **optimal transport** and **Schrödinger bridges**. I am particularly interested in developing learning objectives that are robust, efficient, and mathematically interpretable.

Before focusing on this area, I also worked on **Bayesian optimization for machine learning models**. Alongside my graduate study, I am also pursuing a second degree in **Mathematics** at **Dalat University**. Earlier in my academic path, I completed a degree in **Finance and Banking** at **University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City (UEH)**, which gave me a broader quantitative background and shaped my interdisciplinary perspective on modeling and data-driven research.

My long-term goal is to contribute to the theoretical understanding of modern generative methods and to design principled algorithms that connect elegant mathematical ideas with practical machine learning systems.
